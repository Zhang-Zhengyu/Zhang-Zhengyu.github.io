1        The definition of service leadership

Service leadership is a leadership style that emphasizes serving others first before self-interest (Chung, 2011). It involves building strong relationships, fostering teamwork, and empowering others to achieve their full potential. Service leaders are focused on creating value for their stakeholders, including customers, employees, and the community (Chung, 2011). They lead by example, exhibit empathy, and are committed to continuous improvement.

In this service learning, the beneficiary is not only the recipient of the service, but also the provider of the service (Furco, 1996). Service learning enhances learning on topics related to a specific learning domain through hands-on experience.

2        The Qualities Needed in Effective Service Leadership.

2.1        Moral Character

Morality has great significance in promoting positive character traits and fostering responsible behavior (Berkowitz, 2002). Good personal ethics can promote pro-social behavior. Service leaders guide people to provide services in accordance with social norms and ethical codes. From my perspective, only when a person has good moral standards can he serve others and himself.

Service needs professional skills or other ability that can be served to the recipient. 3        The way that I and other engineering students integrate personal experience and academic discipline in service leadership.

Engineering students have a unique opportunity to integrate their academic discipline to prepare themselves to be service leaders. I can leverage my technical knowledge and skills to create innovative solutions that address real-world problems and improve the quality of life for others. Engineering students can apply design thinking, problem-solving, and project management skills to create value for their stakeholders.

 

To prepare themselves to be service leaders, I, an engineering students can take the following steps:

1.     Develop a service mindset: Engineering students should develop a service mindset that puts the needs of others first. They should be empathetic and seek to understand the problems and challenges faced by their stakeholders.

2.     Seek out opportunities to serve: Engineering students should seek out opportunities to serve their community, such as volunteering, internships, and service-learning projects. These experiences will help them develop leadership skills and gain practical experience in serving others.

3.     Collaborate with others: Engineering students should collaborate with others, including other disciplines and stakeholders, to create innovative solutions that address complex problems.

4.     Focus on continuous improvement: Engineering students should be committed to continuous improvement and seek out feedback from others to improve their leadership skills and effectiveness.

5.     Embrace diversity and inclusion: Engineering students should embrace diversity and inclusion and seek to create an inclusive environment that values different perspectives and experiences.

 

In summary, service leadership is a leadership style that emphasizes serving others first before self-interest. I can integrate my academic discipline to prepare myself to be service leaders by developing a service mindset, seeking out opportunities to serve, collaborating with others, focusing on continuous improvement, and embracing diversity and inclusion. By doing so, I can make a positive impact on my stakeholders and contribute to creating a better world.
